I grew up in the age of online journals. I remember being on Xanga, LiveJournal, and DeadJournal. Social media back then was more limited to your friends and peers and the occasional random person who happened to find you in whatever communities you frequented. We spilled all the beans online. (Who else misses the good ol' days of AIM away messages?)

As time went on I stopped sharing. Part of that was "growing up" and I started feeling a little silly to pour my heart out online. The bigger part, I think, is that I lost who I was and felt I had nothing to share. That my opinions weren't important. Impostor Syndrome would scream who would listen to me or care?

I spent a lot of my life being told who I was. I never felt able to speak my true opinion or feeling because it was either ridiculed, dismissed, or ignored. I was often told I was being dramatic or that I talked too much. Forgive me for wanting to share!

I started keeping things to myself. This was also a problem because, as I have learned over the last few years, when you only keep things in your head, they can be very hard to articulate. They can also start to fester and weigh you down.

I realize that I had split into so many versions of myself because I never felt I could be my full, authentic self all the time. I'd have to pick and choose the parts of me that I shared fearing rejection if I showed it all.

The past two years have been a tremendous period of growth for me. After having my daughter in 2020, I knew I had to make a change. Last year I changed jobs, started therapy, and have learned a lot about myself. I learned that I am valuable, and what I have to say is also valuable.

So that is why I am starting this blog. To finally open up, be my full authentic self, and share the things that interest me unfiltered. To release the demons by sharing my journey and what I am doing to heal and grow. My hope is that I can help or inspire someone else as others have inspired me by sharing their stories.

Thanks for reading.
